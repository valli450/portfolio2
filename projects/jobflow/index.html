Case study

JobFlow

A contractor's entire back office, rebuilt for a phone in a work truck. Projects, invoices, deposits, expenses, and crew, all moving together in real time.

Mobile-first platform Designed and built by Val Dranov Live interactive demo

The problem

Small trade businesses run six-figure operations out of text threads, paper receipts, and memory. A roofer who bills $400k a year often cannot say, on any given Tuesday, who owes them money or whether a job is still profitable.

The software that does exist was built for dispatch offices. It is priced per seat, assumes everyone has a desk, and asks a crew member to create an account before they can check off a task. JobFlow starts from the opposite end: the phone in the truck.

Three decisions that shaped it

Two front doors

Owners sign in with Google and see money. Crews punch a 4-digit PIN handed out by the boss and see today's tasks. No accounts, no email invitations, no app-store friction. A new hire is working inside the app in under a minute. Try it: the PIN door is one tap away in the demo.

Deposits are not an afterthought

Every invoice asks one question: how much of this do you collect before work starts? Half up front, the full amount, or bill now and collect on completion. Cash flow is the number one killer of small trade businesses, so it sits inside the send flow, not in a settings page.

A tax reserve that is always visible

JobFlow quietly holds 25% of net profit in a reserve line on the dashboard. It is the difference between an April surprise and a non-event, and it required exactly zero effort from the owner.

What the demo shows

Everything in the phone is a working interface with live state, not a video. Send an invoice with a 50% deposit and watch it appear in the open list. Record the payment and the dashboard's collected, profit, and tax numbers all move. Log an expense against a job and its budget bar creeps forward.

This demo is a faithful web recreation of the product experience, staged with realistic data. The production app is in private use with real crews today.

Under the hood

Mobile-first interface
Thumb-reach layout: tab bar, bottom sheets, oversized targets for gloved hands.
Role-based access
Owner and crew get different apps from the same data, gated by role.
A single ledger
Every dashboard number derives from payments and expenses. Nothing is stored twice, so nothing drifts.
Crew coordination
Tasks, clock-ins, and site photos flow from the field back to the office live.
Receipts as photos
Snap at the register, tag the job, done. Bookkeeping happens at the point of purchase.
Cash-flow by design
Deposits, payment reminders, and tax reserves are product features, not reports.
